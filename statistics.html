Current Legal Status

The country is primarily split over what to do with the drug with a majority of states having it either fully illegal or decriminalized and/or medical use legalized. However, by reclassifying the substance down from Schedule 1 it would allow for greater amounts of medical research. This research would allow for less legal loopholes to conduct research and more effective uses and treatments using cannabis or THC. 1

Support for Legalization

In the early years of the drug war, a vast majority of people were against legalization. In recent years, the dynamic has switched and most people in the US support full legalization efforts, up to 55% in 2014. However, although most support full legalization, reclassification or decriminalization is a good first step towards this goal that is significantly more politically viable than full legalization. 2

Potential Marijuana Market

Since they have legalized marijuana, Colorado has significantly increased the amount of money raised for their state education programs, more than $40 million. They have also added a large amount to health care, monitoring health benefits, health education, substance abuse treatment and law enforcement just from sales tax on recreational and medicinal use. This graph predicts rapid growth for the Massachusetts marijuana market for both medical and recreational use. If this were on the federal level, a massive amount of debt could be eliminated as a result. 3
